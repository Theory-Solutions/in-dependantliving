# FOUNDERS AGREEMENT — In-dependent Living / Theory Solutions LLC

**⚠️ NOTICE: This is a framework outline for discussion. Have a licensed attorney draft the final document.**

---

## WHY THIS MATTERS

A Founders Agreement answers the hard questions *before* money or success makes them contentious:
- Who owns what percentage?
- What happens if someone leaves?
- Who makes final decisions?
- What is each person's role and expected contribution?
- What happens if someone wants to sell their share?

If In-dependent Living becomes valuable (and it will), a clear agreement protects everyone — including friendships.

## SECTION 1: OWNERSHIP STRUCTURE

### 1.1 IP Ownership
All intellectual property related to In-dependent Living — including source code, designs, trademarks, trade secrets, and business processes — is exclusively owned by **Theory Solutions LLC**.

### 1.2 Equity Allocation

**Principle:** Equity should reflect long-term contribution, risk taken, and capital invested — not just who had the idea.

---

## SECTION 2: VESTING SCHEDULE

Equity should vest over time to protect the company if someone leaves early.

### Recommended: 4-year vesting with 1-year cliff

- **Year 1 (cliff):** 25% of equity vests after 12 months of active contribution
- **Years 2-4:** Remaining 75% vests monthly (1/36 per month)
- **If someone leaves before the cliff:** They receive 0% of unvested equity
- **If the company is acquired before full vesting:** Accelerated vesting (negotiate)

## SECTION 4: DECISION MAKING

### 4.1 Day-to-Day Decisions
CEO has authority for all operational decisions under $[X] (e.g., $5,000).

### 4.2 Major Decisions (require majority founder agreement)
- Raising outside investment
- Hiring full-time employees
- Signing contracts over $[X]
- Pivoting the core product
- Accepting an acquisition offer

### 4.3 Deadlock Resolution
If founders cannot agree on a major decision:
1. 30-day cooling off period
2. Mediation by a mutually agreed neutral party
3. If still unresolved: [binding arbitration / CEO has tiebreaker / buyout triggered]

---

## SECTION 5: FOUNDER DEPARTURE

### 5.1 Voluntary Departure
- Departing founder retains only **vested equity**
- Unvested equity returns to the company pool
- Company has **right of first refusal** on the departing founder's shares

### 5.2 Involuntary Removal (for cause)
- Requires majority founder vote
- May result in accelerated forfeiture of unvested equity
- "Cause" defined as: fraud, gross negligence, criminal conviction, material breach of this agreement

### 5.3 Death or Incapacity
- Shares may transfer to estate, but operational rights stay with remaining founders
- Company has option to purchase shares from estate at fair market value

---

## SECTION 6: TRANSFER RESTRICTIONS (LOCK-UP)

No founder may sell, transfer, or pledge their equity to any third party without:
1. First offering it to other founders (right of first refusal)
2. Obtaining unanimous founder consent
3. If seeking outside investors: board/majority approval required

**Lock-up period:** No transfers for the first **[2 years]** without unanimous consent.

---

## SECTION 7: CONFIDENTIALITY & NON-COMPETE

Each founder agrees to:

**Confidentiality:** Not disclose any proprietary information about In-dependent Living, its technology, users, or business strategy — during or after their involvement.

**Non-compete:** During their active involvement and for **[12-24 months]** after departure, not directly compete by building or joining a competing independent living / senior monitoring application.

**Non-solicitation:** For **[12 months]** after departure, not solicit employees, contractors, or customers of In-dependent Living / Theory Solutions LLC.

---

## SECTION 8: COMPENSATION

### Pre-Revenue
- All founders contribute without salary until the company reaches $[X] monthly revenue
- Reasonable expenses reimbursed with receipts

### Post-Revenue
- Salaries set by mutual agreement, benchmarked to market rate for the role
- Profit distributions per equity percentage after reinvestment reserve

---

## SECTION 9: INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY ASSIGNMENT

Each founder explicitly assigns to Theory Solutions LLC:
- All code written for In-dependent Living
- All designs, wireframes, and creative work
- Any inventions, innovations, or improvements related to the product
- Any work created on company time or using company resources

This assignment is effective immediately and survives departure.

---

## SECTION 10: EXIT SCENARIOS

### Acquisition
- Proceeds distributed per equity percentages after debts
- All founders must cooperate with due diligence
- If one founder blocks a majority-approved acquisition: [drag-along rights apply]

### IPO
- Founders subject to standard 180-day lockup post-IPO
- Registration rights to be negotiated

### Shutdown
- Assets liquidated, debts paid, remainder distributed per equity
